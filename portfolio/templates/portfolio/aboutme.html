About me

From self-taught coder to coding teacher, if your development team is looking for individuals who have a strong ability to motivate themselves and be self-starters, then look no further.

search

As I always told my students, most professional fields are not defined by what you know; instead, they are defined by your ability to accept that you don't know something and proceed to figure out the answer. Back in 2018, I started my first college-level computer science course, which aimed to teach the fundamentals of coding with C# in Unity's game development engine. I was immediately hooked, but due to financial concerns and a number of other factors, I withdrew from my classes and began teaching myself. I was a broke college student, so I went to the library, grabbed some free resources, booted up my 2012 MacBook Pro, and began studying. Since then, I have gone on to teach adult classes in Python, helping them get certified in their own Python journey. I have also taught C# with Unity classes of my own. I have a lot of passion for software development, and it's what helped me excel in my position as the head of curriculum at Code Wiz Durham.

I believe that the power is not in the knowledge someone has, but in their ability to never stop trying to learn new things. My work reflects that, as I am often seeking new avenues to learn new things. Most recently, I got certified in backend development with Python's Django and Django REST framework. I have a strong focus on making responsive and efficient websites tailored to clients' needs.

Lastly, I consider myself to be a fast learner. In two months, I went from having very little knowledge of backend development to creating and deploying this website (and my musical bingo website concept) with Django, PostgreSQL, Docker, and Heroku. If you made it this far, you may be wondering, "Why was there Japanese on the home page?" The answer is because I am fluent in Japanese after 4 years of self-guided, dedicated studying. 私のサイトへようこそ！
